## Axis-Angle Rotation

Given a rotation axis represented by a normalized vector $u = (u_1, u_2, u_3) \in \mathbb{R}^3$ and a rotation angle $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$, the corresponding rotation matrix $R_u(\alpha) \in \mathrm{SO}(3)$ is defined as:

$$
R_u(\alpha) = \cos(\alpha) \cdot I + (1 - \cos(\alpha)) \cdot uu^T + \sin(\alpha) \cdot U_\times
$$

Where $U_\times$ is the **skew-symmetric matrix**:

$$
U_\times = 
\begin{pmatrix}
0 & -u_3 & u_2 \\
u_3 & 0 & -u_1 \\
-u_2 & u_1 & 0
\end{pmatrix}
$$

Properties of $R_u$:

* $R_u(0) = I$
* $R_u^{-1}(\alpha) = R_u(-\alpha)$
* $R_u(\alpha) = R_{-u}(-\alpha)$

This representation uses only **4 parameters** (angle + axis), but animating both the axis and the angle can still be unintuitive.
